Digital Forensics

About

Digital forensics is a critical component of national security in the cyber world. It involves the investigation and analysis of digital devices to uncover evidence of cybercrimes and security breaches. Computer science plays a vital role in developing advanced tools and techniques for digital forensics, enabling experts to recover data, trace cyber attacks, and identify perpetrators.

By using concealed credit card skimmers, hackers can steal sensitive financial information from unsuspecting victims. These skimmers use inexpensive bluetooth low energy (BLE) technology combined with Python-based scripts to transmit stolen data wirelessly to the attacker. These low cost devices exploit vulnerabilities in point-of-sale systems, posing significant threats to national security.

So what tools are used in digital forensics to combat these threats?

  • Wireshark is used to inspect BLE payloads and to fingerprint configurations
  • Ubertooth One is used as passive BLE sniffing to capture advertising traffic
  • Script monitoring flags when BLE advertising stops, indicating a live conection to a skimmer
